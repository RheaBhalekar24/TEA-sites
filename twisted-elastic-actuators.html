Core Technology

Twisted Elastic Actuators (TEAs)

Twisted elastic actuators are a soft-actuation extension of twisted-string systems. Instead of rigid strings, they use elastic cords that both transmit motion and provide compliance.

How they work

A motor rotates and twists one or more elastic strands. As the strands twist, they shorten and pull on the load path, creating force and displacement. Because the medium is elastic, the system can also absorb shock and store energy.

Why TEAs are useful for wearable robotics

  • Built-in compliance supports safer human-robot interaction.
  • Actively tunable stiffness helps adapt to different gait or task phases.
  • Impact damping protects drivetrain components.
  • Energy storage and release can improve cyclic-motion efficiency.
  • Softer transmission can reduce perceived bulk and rigidity for users.

Relevance to the Soft Knee Exoskeleton project

In this thesis, TEAs are explored as an alternative to fully rigid lower-limb actuation. The focus is on making knee assistance systems more compact, adaptable, and comfortable while preserving useful force output characteristics.
